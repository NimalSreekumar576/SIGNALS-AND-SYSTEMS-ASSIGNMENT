\textbf{Question 11.9.5.32:}
150 workers were engaged to finish a job in a certain number of days, 4 workers dropped out on second day, 4 more workers dropped out on third day and so on. It took 8 more days to finish the work. Find the number of days in which the work was completed.\\

\solution\\
\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\input{table1.tex}
\caption{Input Parameters}
\end{table}

Let p be the number of days required to complete the work when all 150 workers work continuously for p days.
\begin{align}
\implies \text{total work done} = 150p
\end{align}

Given that after first day, 4 workers starts leaving each day. This forms an A.P. with
\begin{align}
x\brak{n} &= \brak{150-4n}u\brak{n}\\
X\brak{z} &= \frac{150}{1-z^{-1}}- \frac{4z^{-1}}{\brak{1-z^{-1}}^2}\\
y\brak{n} &= x\brak{n}*u\brak{n}\\
Y\brak{z} &= X\brak{z}U\brak{z}\\
Y\brak{z} &=\frac{150}{\brak{1-z^{-1}}^2}- \frac{4z^{-1}}{\brak{1-z^{-1}}^3}
\end{align}\\


Using the z transforms given below:
\begin{align}
\brak{n+1}u\brak{n} \xleftrightarrow{z} \frac{1}{\brak{1-z^{-1}}^2} \qquad ,|z| > 1 \\
n\brak{\brak{n+1}u\brak{n}}\xleftrightarrow{z} \frac{2z^{-1}}{\brak{1-z^{-1}}^3} \qquad ,|z| > 1 
\end{align}

\begin{align}
\implies y\brak{n} &= 150\brak{n+1}u\brak{n} - 2n\brak{\brak{n+1}u\brak{n}}\\
y\brak{n} &= \brak{148n - 2n^2 + 150}u\brak{n}
\end{align}



And its given in the question that it takes 8 additional \brak{p+8} days to complete the work when 4 workers start dropping out each day.
\begin{align}
\implies \text{total work done} = y\brak{p+7}
\end{align}

Equating eq\brak{1} and eq\brak{11}

\begin{align}
120p - 2p^2 +1088 &= 150p\\
\brak{p-17}\brak{p+32} &= 0 \\
p &= 17,-32 
\end{align}

No. of days cannot be negative $\implies p=17$ \\
Total no. of days it took to complete work = p+8= 25\\
